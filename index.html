The Vision

Our vision is simple — cut the cost of flight and go electric, with hydrogen-powered aircraft, to make flight the default way humanity moves.

~30%
Of what it costs to fly is fuel — aviation's single largest expense, and the one number no operator controls.
~3%
Net margin for the global airline industry in its best years — across its history it has barely broken even, because fuel swings eat the rest.
0
Exposure to the oil market. Hydrogen-electric energy is produced, not traded — a stable, predictable cost per flight for the first time.

The world accepts that flying is just expensive. We don't. We make our own hydrogen from the limitless energy of the sun — no oil, no fossil fuel, no price set by anyone else. Strip a flight to first principles and its cost is fuel, maintenance and crew: a hydrogen-electric powertrain runs on energy we produce, has almost no moving parts, and flies unmanned. We build the airframe, the powertrain and the operation ourselves — so the cost per flight is lower, predictable, and finally ours. That is the whole company.

Phase 1 · We are here

Rebuild the aircraft.

~62% of the cost of a flight

We attack the machine first. Hydrogen-electric drops the energy bill to a fraction of ATF. An electric drivetrain cuts the maintenance cost. Unmanned flight removes the crew entirely. The hardest part of the problem — and the part the Indian Navy is backing us to build.

Phase 2 · Next

Own the runways.

~22% of the cost of a flight

Once the aircraft is proven, we stop renting space in congested mega-hubs. Company-owned short runways sited next to factories and industrial clusters erase landing fees, slot queues and the hub in the middle — and shorten the journey door to door.

Phase 3 · Then

Fly the fleet ourselves.

The whole flight, captured

With the cost of flying rebuilt from the ground up, we operate the aircraft as our own airline — our fleet, our runways, the full economics captured end to end.

We start with the hardest part — the aircraft. The Indian Navy is supporting us to build it as a dual-use ISR and ship-resupply platform. Figures are illustrative of a domestic narrowbody leg; working estimates, not a quote.
